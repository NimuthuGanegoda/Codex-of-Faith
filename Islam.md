# Islam: Submission to the One God

## Historical Context
Islam was revealed in the 7th century CE to the Prophet Muhammad in Mecca. The faith is centered on the **Quran**, believed to be the literal word of God (Allah).

## The Five Pillars of Islam (Arkan al-Islam)
These are the foundational practices of the faith:
1. **Shahada (Profession of Faith):** "There is no god but God, and Muhammad is the Messenger of God."
2. **Salat (Daily Prayer):** Performing five daily prayers facing the Kaaba in Mecca.
3. **Zakat (Almsgiving):** Giving a portion of one's wealth (usually 2.5%) to the poor.
4. **Sawm (Fasting):** Abstaining from food and drink during the daylight hours of the month of **Ramadan**.
5. **Hajj (Pilgrimage):** Making a journey to the holy city of Mecca at least once in a lifetime, if physically and financially able.

## Core Beliefs
* **Monotheism (Tawhid):** Strict belief in the absolute oneness of God.
* **Prophets:** Belief in a long line of prophets, including Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, with Muhammad being the final prophet.
* **Day of Judgment:** Belief in accountability for one's actions in the afterlife.

## The Ummah
Islam emphasizes the unity of the global community of believers (*Ummah*), regardless of race or status.
