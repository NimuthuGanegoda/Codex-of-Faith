# Hinduism: Sanatana Dharma (The Eternal Way)

## Introduction
Hinduism is one of the world's oldest religions, with no single founder. It evolved over millennia in the Indian subcontinent and is often described as a way of life rather than a structured creed.

## The Sacred Texts
* **The Vedas:** The oldest and most authoritative scriptures, considered "heard" (*Shruti*) revelations. There are four: Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda.
* **The Upanishads:** Philosophical texts that explore the nature of reality and the soul, teaching that the individual soul (**Atman**) is one with the ultimate reality (**Brahman**).
* **The Epics:** The *Ramayana* and the *Mahabharata* (which includes the *Bhagavad Gita*) provide moral and spiritual guidance through narrative.

## Core Concepts
* **Dharma:** The moral order, duty, and righteousness that upholds the universe. It is the "right way of living."
* **Karma:** The law of cause and effect. Every action has a corresponding consequence, determining the quality of future lives.
* **Samsara:** The continuous cycle of birth, death, and rebirth.
* **Moksha:** The ultimate goal—liberation from *Samsara* and the merging of the soul with Brahman.

## Practices and Deities
Hinduism is characterized by a vast diversity of practices and the worship of many deities, all seen as manifestations of the one Brahman. Major deities include:
* **Brahma:** The Creator.
* **Vishnu:** The Preserver.
* **Shiva:** The Destroyer (of ego and ignorance).
* **Devi:** The Divine Mother.

## Conclusion
Hinduism emphasizes the pursuit of truth and the fulfillment of one's duties while seeking union with the divine.
