# Sikhism: The Path of Equality and Service

## History
Founded in the 15th century in the Punjab region by **Guru Nanak Dev Ji**. He was followed by nine human Gurus. The tenth, Guru Gobind Singh Ji, designated the **Guru Granth Sahib** (holy scripture) as the eternal living Guru.

## Core Tenets
* **Ik Onkar:** Belief in one Supreme Reality, the creator of all.
* **Equality:** Absolute equality of all humans, rejecting caste, creed, and gender discrimination.
* **Selfless Service (Seva):** Helping others without expectation of reward.

## The Three Pillars
1. **Naam Japo:** Meditating on God's name.
2. **Kirat Karni:** Earning an honest and truthful living.
3. **Vand Chakko:** Sharing with others and helping those in need.

## The Five Ks (Articles of Faith)
Initiated Sikhs (Khalsa) wear five symbols of commitment:
* **Kesh:** Uncut hair (symbolizing acceptance of God's will).
* **Kara:** A steel bracelet.
* **Kanga:** A wooden comb.
* **Kachera:** Cotton undergarments.
* **Kirpan:** A ceremonial sword (symbolizing the duty to protect the weak).

## Conclusion
Sikhism is a faith of action, emphasizing internal devotion and external service to humanity.
