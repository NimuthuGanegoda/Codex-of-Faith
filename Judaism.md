# Judaism: The Covenant of the People

## History and Origins
Judaism is one of the oldest monotheistic religions, originating over 3,500 years ago. It is based on a **Covenant** (sacred agreement) between God and the Jewish people, starting with Abraham and later Moses.

## Core Beliefs
* **Monotheism:** Belief in one, indivisible God.
* **The Torah:** The foundational text (the first five books of the Hebrew Bible) containing divine laws and teachings.
* **Mitzvot:** 613 commandments that guide ethical behavior and daily life.
* **Tikkun Olam:** The obligation to "repair the world" through social justice and kindness.

## Practices
* **Sabbath (Shabbat):** A weekly day of rest and spiritual focus from Friday sunset to Saturday sunset.
* **Synagogue:** The place of worship and community gathering.
* **High Holy Days:** **Rosh Hashanah** (New Year) and **Yom Kippur** (Day of Atonement).

## Conclusion
Judaism emphasizes action and ethical living in this world, maintaining a deep connection to history and tradition.
